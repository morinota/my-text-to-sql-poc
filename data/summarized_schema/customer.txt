The "customer" table contains data related to customers associated with a store. The columns include:

- **customer_id**: A unique identifier for each customer (primary key).
- **store_id**: Identifier for the store to which the customer belongs (foreign key).
- **first_name**: The first name of the customer.
- **last_name**: The last name of the customer.
- **email**: The email address of the customer.
- **address_id**: Identifier for the address of the customer (foreign key).
- **active**: Status indicating if the customer is active.
- **create_date**: The date the customer record was created.
- **last_update**: The date the customer record was last updated.

This table can be used to analyze customer demographics, track customer engagement, and assess store performance. Potential use cases include:

1. Identifying active and inactive customers for marketing strategies.
2. Analyzing customer information by store to evaluate store performance.
3. Examining customer creation trends over time to inform recruitment of customer-facing staff.
4. Tracking changes in customer data over time for reporting and auditing purposes.
5. Aggregating customer data for loyalty program analysis or customer retention efforts.
